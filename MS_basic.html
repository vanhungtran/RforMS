MASS SPECTROMETRY

❖ PRINCIPLES, INSTRUMENTATION, & APPLICATIONS ❖

1. Principle

Mass spectrometry (MS) is an analytical technique used to measure the mass-to-charge ratio (m/z) of ions. It helps in identifying, quantifying, and characterizing molecules based on their mass, structure, and abundance.

Key Equation:
m/z = Mass of ion (m) / Charge on ion (z)
⚡ Higher m/z → Heavier mass
⚡ Higher m/z → Lower charge

  • Inlet System: Introduces sample into the instrument (GC, LC, syringe pump).
  • Ion Source: Converts analyte molecules into gas-phase ions.
  • Mass Analyzer: Separates ions based on their unique m/z ratio.
  • Detector: Detects separated ions and converts them to electrical signals.

4. Ionization Techniques

Electron Ionization (EI): High energy electrons bombard volatile samples. Hard ionization; produces significant fragmentations. Used for GC-MS.
Electrospray Ionization (ESI): Soft ionization technique used for polar/large molecules (LC-MS). Produces multiply charged ions.
MALDI: Matrix-Assisted Laser Desorption Ionization. Soft laser desorption method ideal for large proteins and polymers.

5. Mass Analyzers (Work on m/z Principle)

Analyzer Underlying Principle Primary Features / Common Uses
Quadrupole (Q) Ions pass through oscillating RF/DC fields; only specific m/z values match a stable trajectory. Robust, fast scanning, low cost. Extremely widely adopted in routine GC-MS and LC-MS.
Time of Flight (TOF) Ions accelerated with uniform kinetic energy; velocity depends strictly on mass over a fixed drift path. High resolution, accurate mass capabilities, theoretically unlimited mass range. Used in MALDI-TOF.
Ion Trap (IT) Ions are dynamic/statically trapped in 3D or linear fields, then sequentially ejected by scanning frequency. Highly sensitive, compact structural footprint. Capable of complex MSn tandem fragmentation.
Orbitrap / FT-ICR Ions oscillate electrostatically around a central spindle or move inside strong superconducting magnetic fields. Ultra-high resolution and unmatched mass accuracy. Reserved for advanced research fields.

  • 🎯 Base Peak: Most intense peak in spectrum, arbitrarily assigned 100% abundance.
  • 🎯 Molecular Ion (M•+): Radical cation representing intact molecular weight.
  • 🎯 Fragment Ions: Lower-mass fragments indicating structural architecture.
  • 🎯 Isotopic Peaks: Secondary footprints due to natural isotope distributions (e.g., 13C).

7. Types of Mass Spectrometry

🌐 GC-MS: Gas Chromatography coupled with MS; ideal for volatile compounds.
🌐 LC-MS: Liquid Chromatography coupled with MS; for non-volatile, polar compounds.
🌐 MS/MS (Tandem MS): Sequential mass filters isolate, fragment, and re-analyze ions.
🌐 MALDI-TOF MS: Specialized system optimized for high molecular weight biological polymers.
🌐 ICP-MS: Inductively Coupled Plasma MS; trace elemental analysis down to parts-per-trillion.

12. Advantages vs. Limitations

Advantages (✓)
  • Ultra-high sensitivity (fmol)
  • Exceptional target selectivity
  • Structural elucidation depth
  • High-throughput integration
Limitations (✗)
  • High instrument cost
  • Requires skilled personnel
  • Susceptible to matrix effects
  • Destructive to samples
KEY TERMS:
• m/z: mass-to-charge ratio
• Resolution: power to separate narrow peaks
