17  Post-translational Modification Analysis

Post-translational modifications (PTMs) are chemical alterations to proteins that profoundly regulate biological function. Mass spectrometry is the primary technology for PTM discovery and site localization. This chapter covers phosphoproteomics as the flagship PTM application, with extensions to other common modifications.

17.1 Overview

PTMs add or remove mass from specific amino acid residues. MS-based PTM analysis requires:

  1. Enrichment — selectively capturing modified peptides
  2. Database search with variable modifications enabled
  3. Site localization — determining the exact modified residue
  4. Quantification — measuring modification stoichiometry across conditions

17.2 Common PTMs in MS-Based Proteomics

PTM Residue(s) Mass Shift (Da) Function
Phosphorylation S, T, Y +79.966 Signalling
Acetylation K, N-term +42.011 Chromatin, metabolism
Ubiquitination K +114.043 (GlyGly) Protein degradation
Methylation K, R +14.016 Epigenetics
Oxidation M +15.995 Oxidative stress
Deamidation N, Q +0.984 Sample degradation artefact
